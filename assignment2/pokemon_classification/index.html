EDA-Informed Preprocessing Decisions
  • 📊 Extreme long-tail distribution — 898 classes, avg 2.79 img/class. Only 378 classes have ≥ 3 images, enabling a meaningful 3-way split.
  • 🔍 Cross-class duplicate detected — Appletun ↔ Flapple shared an identical MD5 hash. The duplicate was removed before splitting to prevent label leakage.
  • 🖼️ All images are JPEG / RGB, no corrupted files — uniform preprocessing with ImageNet normalization (μ=[0.485,0.456,0.406], σ=[0.229,0.224,0.225]) applied directly.
  • 📐 Aspect-ratio outliers present in EDA → square-resize to 224 × 224 (EfficientNet-B0 native size) to avoid distortion.
  • ⚖️ Split coverage risk — a random split left 659 classes with no validation sample. Replaced with a stratified per-class split (70/15/15) guaranteeing every class appears in all three partitions.

Images-per-Class Distribution (After Filtering)

After applying the ≥ 3 images/class threshold, 378 classes remain out of 898 (42%). The distribution is still left-skewed: 139 classes have exactly 3 images and 150 have 4, confirming the extreme data scarcity that drives the choice of pretrained features over end-to-end training.

Approach A — Feature Extraction + Classical ML

Pipeline
Image → Resize 224×224 → Normalize → EfficientNet-B0 [frozen] → 1280-dim feature → StandardScaler → Classifier

The EfficientNet-B0 backbone (ImageNet-1k, 4.49M total params) is fully frozen. The adaptive-average-pooled feature vector (1280 dimensions) serves as the input to three classical ML classifiers. All features are standardised (zero mean, unit variance) on training data before classification.

    Discussion
    • 🏆 Approach A (Logistic Regression) wins overall with 64.55% Top-1 and 84.39% Top-5 accuracy. Despite being a linear classifier, it benefits enormously from rich 1280-dim EfficientNet embeddings in a few-shot setting where fine-tuning is data-starved.
    • 📉 Fine-tuning underperforms (28.84% Top-1). With only ~2 training images per class, end-to-end adaptation overfits quickly, even with label smoothing (0.05), dropout (0.35), and aggressive data augmentation. The model learns to memorise rather than generalise.
    • 🔧 SVM (RBF) underperforms Logistic Regression despite its theoretical advantage in high-dimensional spaces. With so few training samples (761 total), the kernel's flexibility becomes a liability — it struggles to find a reliable decision boundary.
    • 🎯 Top-5 is a more meaningful metric in this extreme few-shot setting. k-NN achieves 79.89% Top-5 accuracy — meaning the correct class is within the 5 nearest neighbours for most test images, even though Top-1 precision is modest at 34%.
    • 📊 Bimodal per-class accuracy distribution for the best model: 244 classes achieve 100% accuracy (single test sample, correct prediction) while 134 classes achieve 0% (often visually similar species with scarce training data).
    Recommendations for Future Work
    • 🌐Data augmentation at collection time — scrape additional Pokémon images to reduce the long-tail and increase per-class sample size.
    • 🧠Few-shot learning methods (Prototypical Networks, MAML) are architecturally better suited for this distribution than standard softmax fine-tuning.
    • 🔬Self-supervised pre-training on unlabelled Pokémon images (DINO, SimCLR) before supervised fine-tuning to bridge the domain gap from ImageNet.
    • 📏Cosine-similarity or nearest-centroid classifiers on the 1280-dim embeddings often outperform full softmax in few-shot regimes.
